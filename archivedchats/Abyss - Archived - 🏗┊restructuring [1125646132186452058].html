Deleted User 7/4/2023 3:43 PM
Few thoughts and ideas: Gold is earned for personal reward and discourages real engagement. The fact that we basically pay people to do work does not help with our mission at all. We are basically encouraging the credit grind by having this system in place. Instead of helping the guild and gaining experience as leaders, members are encouraged to take a step back and focus on earning gold for personal reward. This is extremely counter-intuitive considering our current goal. We should put less emphasis on credit requirements. Some members have it in their head that the credit requirement is the primary requirement for attaining a position in Abyss. We should remember what credit tracks in the first place--player reputation. The reputation of a player is only one of many things to consider when promoting them, and so it shouldn't be treated as a primary requirement in any regard. We should take a step back and evaluate players based on the other requirements. If we establish that the credit requirement is merely the first step towards attaining a role, then we will shift the focus from grinding credit to actually meeting the real requirements of the position. Drastically reducing the credit requirements should also help. Players won't be denied positions because of how much credit they have, but because they meet or don't meet genuine requirements. And because credit is now less important when evaluating players, this enables adult players who don't have the time to grind all the credit, but do have the maturity and do meet the other requirements of a position. We could also consider simply removing all credit requirements on paper only. The credit system is just a tool that allows us to factor in player reputation when making judgements. The actual credit itself doesn't matter. It should only ever influence--not decide--our overall judgement. Also, just now as I'm writing this, I think it really cements my point that when Shunsy posted that image of guild ranks, he wrote down the credit amounts required for each rank--as if that is the most important thing for each rank--like the primary requirement.
